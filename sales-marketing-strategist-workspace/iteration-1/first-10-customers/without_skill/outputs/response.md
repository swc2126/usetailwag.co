# TailWag Go-to-Market Plan: Acquiring Your First 10 Customers

## Executive Summary

You're launching an AI-powered SMS platform solving a real pain point for dog daycares: automating communication with pet owners. Your first 10 customers will come from direct, relationship-based outreach targeting daycares in the DFW metroplex and surrounding regions where you can provide white-glove onboarding.

**Timeline:** 6-8 weeks from launch to first 10 customers
**Target segment:** Independent dog daycares (20-100 dogs/day)
**Primary channel:** Direct outreach + warm introductions
**Success metric:** Signed contracts with initial 10 customers

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch Preparation (Weeks 1-2)

### 1.1 Build Your Core Assets

**Sales Demo Package**
- Your three demo modules (report cards, reminders, review requests) are your MVP sales tool
- Create a 5-minute screen recording showing each feature in action
- Prepare a sample report card showing a real daycare's dog (fictional) with personalized personality-driven messaging
- Record yourself giving a live 15-minute walkthrough that prospects can watch before meetings

**One-Pager / Pitch Deck**
- Create a simple one-page PDF: Problem (owner/staff communication gaps) → Solution (TailWag automation) → Results (time saved, reviews generated, owner satisfaction)
- Pricing options for different daycare sizes (by number of dogs or SMS volume)
- Suggested ROI calculator showing hours saved per week

**Email Template Library**
- Cold outreach template for daycares you don't know
- Warm introduction follow-up for referral contacts
- Demo request confirmation email
- Post-demo follow-up with custom pricing
- Trial offer email

**Local Branding**
- TailWag's existing brand guidelines are solid—lean on them
- Create a "TailWag for Daycares" landing page if you have a website
- Prepare Instagram/Facebook assets showing the software in action (if you have social channels)

### 1.2 Identify Your Core Prospect List

**Who to target (first 10):**
- Independent dog daycares in DFW (Dallas-Ft. Worth metroplex): ~150+ daycares
- Filter by: 20+ dogs/day capacity, engaged social media presence (high review requests), no existing automated SMS platform
- Geographic priority: Irving, Dallas, Plano, Arlington, Fort Worth (where you have local credibility)

**How to build the list:**
1. Google search: "dog daycare near [city]", "doggie daycare near [city]"
2. Check Google Maps for daycares with 50+ reviews (indicates volume, engaged customer base)
3. Cross-check their websites for current communication approach (if basic/manual, they're good prospects)
4. Use LinkedIn to find owners/managers at target daycares
5. Check Yelp, BarkBox, Rover for additional prospects

**Target at least 100 prospects to reach 10 customers** (typical 10% conversion for first-time enterprise product).

## Phase 2: Outreach Strategy (Weeks 3-5)

### 2.1 Multi-Channel Outreach

**Channel 1: Warm Introductions (40% of pipeline)**
- Ask everyone you know: friends, family, LinkedIn connections if they know anyone at a dog daycare
- Attend local DFW business networking events (Chamber of Commerce, entrepreneur meetups)
- Ask existing TailWag employees/advisors for introductions
- Referrals have 5-7x higher conversion rates than cold outreach

**Channel 2: Direct Cold Email (30% of pipeline)**
- Target decision-maker (owner or operations manager) by name on LinkedIn
- Subject line examples:
  - "Quick question: How do you handle appointment reminders at [Daycare Name]?"
  - "[Daycare Name] + SMS = More Google Reviews? (2-min question)"
  - "Helping DFW daycares save 5-10 hours/week on customer comms"
- Email body:
  - Personalize with specific detail about their daycare (recent review you read, mention their facility)
  - 2-3 sentences max identifying the problem
  - One sentence CTAssistant: "Can I show you a 5-minute demo this week?"
  - Link to your demo video (so they see value before the call)
- Send 10-15 cold emails per week, tracking responses

**Channel 3: Phone/LinkedIn Direct Messages (20% of pipeline)**
- After email sits for 3 days with no response, try LinkedIn DM or phone call
- Keep script simple: "Hi [Name], I sent you an email about TailWag last Thursday. It's a tool that automates SMS to dog owners. Do you have 15 minutes this week to see it in action?"
- Daycares are used to answering phones—don't be shy

**Channel 4: Community Leverage (10% of pipeline)**
- Join local DFW pet/startup Slack communities and Facebook groups
- Share a short testimonial (once you get your first 2-3 customers): "We helped [Daycare] generate 12 Google reviews in their first month"
- Don't hard-sell—answer questions, offer help, build credibility

### 2.2 Outreach Sequence

**Week 3:**
- Send 15 cold emails to top prospects
- Make 5 warm introduction requests
- Post on 2-3 relevant Facebook/community groups

**Week 4:**
- Follow up on cold emails (LinkedIn DM or call)
- Schedule demos from Week 3 responses
- Send 15 more new cold emails
- Attend 1 local networking event

**Week 5:**
- Close first demo meetings
- Continue follow-ups
- Adjust messaging based on early objections

## Phase 3: Sales & Onboarding (Weeks 6-8)

### 3.1 The Demo Call

**Pre-demo:**
- Send calendar invite + link to your 5-minute demo video
- Have them test your demo with their own dog names (if possible) or use their actual dogs
- Ask them to write down 2 dogs they'd like to see report cards for

**During demo (15-20 min):**
1. **Problem validation** (3 min): "Walk me through how you currently send appointment reminders/follow-ups to owners"
2. **Show report card generation** (5 min): Generate a real report card for a dog they mentioned, live
3. **Show reminders + review requests** (5 min): Demonstrate the other two features
4. **Pricing & ROI** (3 min): "We bill per SMS sent. Most daycares your size run $X-Y per month. That replaces about 5-10 hours of manual work"
5. **Next steps** (2 min): "I'd love to get you set up with a 2-week trial. We'll onboard your dogs and staff, send your first batch of messages together, and you'll see the impact"

**Objection handling:**
- "We're fine with email/WhatsApp": Anchor to SMS open rates (98%+ vs 20% email)
- "Can you integrate with our POS system?": "We can do manual dog uploads for now; integrations come later as you grow"
- "What if owners don't respond?" "That's the point—you're automating reminders so owners DON'T have to call back"
- "Will this work with our staff workflow?" "We'll customize the templates to match your brand voice and send times that fit your operations"

### 3.2 Pricing & Offer Structure

**Recommended first 10 customer pricing:**
- Freemium or free trial: 2 weeks free for first 10 customers (builds social proof and case studies)
- Introductory pricing: $99-199/month (based on dog volume: e.g., $99 for <100 dogs/month, $199 for <300 dogs/month)
- Volume pricing hint: "After your trial, this scales from $99 to $299/month as your volume grows"

**Why low entry price for first 10:**
- You need case studies, testimonials, and metrics to close the next 100
- Early customers become your best salespeople (word-of-mouth in tight daycare community)
- Churn risk is low if they're delighted with results

### 3.3 Onboarding Playbook for First 10 Customers

**Week 1 of customer relationship:**
- Schedule a 30-min onboarding call
- Upload their dogs, owner phone numbers, and staff contact info
- Customize message templates to match their brand voice
- Set up first batch of messages to send tomorrow

**Week 2:**
- Monitor first day of message sends together (live call)
- Collect feedback on message quality and timing
- Adjust templates based on feedback
- Show them the first metrics: delivery rate, open rate, response rate

**Weeks 3-4:**
- Monitor review requests sent and new Google/Yelp reviews generated
- Calculate ROI: "You sent 45 review requests this month and got 8 new reviews—that's an 18% review generation rate. Those reviews bring in ~$X in new revenue based on typical pet owner booking patterns"
- Schedule monthly success check-ins
- Ask for a testimonial and permission to use them as a case study

### 3.4 Closing & Contracts

**Trial to paid conversion:**
- After 2-week trial, send a summary email: "Here's what TailWag generated for you"
- Include metrics: messages sent, opens, responses, new reviews
- Propose 3-month paid plan: "Let's continue at [price] per month"
- Make signing simple: one-page service agreement + stripe link to start billing

**Target conversion:** 70% of trial starts → paid customers = 10 trials needed for ~7 paid customers
**Buffer:** Aim for 15 trial starts to land 10 paid customers by week 8

## Phase 4: Building Momentum (Post-Launch)

### 4.1 Case Studies & Testimonials

**For each of your first 3 paying customers, collect:**
- Written testimonial (2-3 sentences on the impact)
- Permission to use their dog photos and daycare name in marketing
- Metrics: hours saved, new reviews generated, owner satisfaction improvement
- A 5-minute video testimonial (optional but powerful)

**Use these immediately:**
- Add to your landing page
- Reference in cold emails to similar-sized daycares
- Share in local DFW business groups and on social media

### 4.2 Referral Program

**Once you hit customer #5, launch a referral program:**
- Offer $200-300 credit (or cash) for each qualified referral that converts
- Ask existing customers to refer a friend at another daycare
- Daycares talk to each other at industry events—leverage this

### 4.3 Metrics to Track Daily

- Emails sent / response rate
- Demo scheduled (from emails)
- Demo conversion to trial
- Trial conversion to paid
- Customer acquisition cost (total outreach cost ÷ new customers)
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)

## Success Markers & Contingencies

### Success looks like:
- Week 4: 5-8 demos scheduled
- Week 5: 2-3 customers in trial
- Week 7: 5-6 customers in trial
- Week 8: 8-10 customers paying (at least 6-month commitments)

### If you're behind:
- Increase outreach volume (send 20+ emails/week instead of 10-15)
- Add more warm intro requests (get friends/advisors to make 2-3 calls each)
- Lower trial barrier: offer 4-week free trial instead of 2-week
- Increase trial-to-paid conversion by adding more onboarding calls (1-2 per week per customer)
- Add a "founder discount": "Sign before April 30 and lock in 40% off for your first year" (creates urgency)

## The Bottom Line

Your first 10 customers will come from **persistence + personalization**, not marketing magic. You're pre-launch, so spend 70% of your time on direct outreach (emails, calls, warm intros) and 30% on building assets and refining pitch. Every conversation teaches you something new about what daycares actually need.

Focus on **speed of learning** over volume in the first 3 weeks: take every demo, listen hard, and adjust. By week 4, you'll have market clarity—then scale the channel that's working fastest.
