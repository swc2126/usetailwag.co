# TailWag Walk-In Demo Prep Guide

## The Strategy: Why This Matters

A walk-in demo is the highest-touch, highest-stakes way to introduce TailWag because it removes friction. The daycare owner sees you, sees the product in action on their own phones, and feels the personal attention that nobody else in the space gives them. This is your competitive advantage — not features, but presence.

The goal isn't to close them on the spot (though if you do, great). The goal is to:
1. **Get them to say "yes, that would help"** — Make them feel seen. Show them you understand the specific pain they're experiencing.
2. **Leave them with something tangible** — A demo that works, a next-step they're excited about, something they'll actually remember.
3. **Be memorable and professional** — You're a solo founder working out of Irving showing up at their door. First impression matters more than pitch perfection.

Walk-ins are unpredictable (they might be busy, skeptical, or swamped), so you need a flexible plan that scales from "2-minute hallway conversation" to "15-minute sit-down demo."

---

## What to Say: The Demo Script (Flexible Version)

### The Opening (30 seconds — no more)

Walk in with a smile, introduce yourself, and lead with curiosity:

> "Hi, I'm Summer — I'm building something for independent daycares in the DFW area. I know you're busy, so I'll be quick: I noticed [specific observation about their Google reviews OR their Instagram OR their business] and I thought of something you might find useful. Do you have 2 minutes right now, or would another time this week be better?"

**Why this works:**
- It's honest (you're not pretending you just happened to be there).
- It shows you researched them (very disarming).
- It respects their time (gives them an out without awkwardness).
- It's friendly, not salesy.

### If They Say "Sure, I have a couple minutes"

Show, don't tell. Pull out your phone and walk through this scenario:

> "So here's the problem I'm solving: Every day, a parent picks up their dog but never hears how the day went. Meanwhile, you just texted their favorite thing that happened — but you did it in your head. What if they got a personalized text that made them smile, without you having to do anything?"

**Then show this flow:**

1. **Open your demo on your phone** — Show the TailWag report card for their daycare (you'll have created this beforehand with realistic dog names from their Instagram or reviews).

2. **Read it out loud** — Example:
   > "Cooper had a tail-wagging day at [Daycare Name]! This morning he was a little shy at pickup, but once the other pups arrived, it was playtime nonstop. He loved the splash pad and was a total gentleman during snack time. He's tuckered out and ready for a cozy evening. See you tomorrow! 🐕"

3. **Pause and ask** — "That text takes you 30 seconds if you write it manually. TailWag generates that in real-time. And here's what matters — parents see their dog mentioned by name, see what they actually did that day, and boom — they remember why they love you."

4. **Swipe to the second feature** — Show an appointment reminder SMS (simple, professional, reduces no-shows).

5. **Swipe to the third feature** — Show a review request that comes at the right time.

> "These three things — report cards, reminders, and review requests — are the annoying manual texts you either do inconsistently or never get to. TailWag automates all of it. It takes five minutes to set up and you can run your whole daycare while it works in the background."

### If They Seem Interested ("This is cool" or "How much?")

Ask the disarming question:

> "Real talk — do you currently send personalized texts to parents about their dog's day?"

Most will say "Not every day" or "I try to when I have time." Then say:

> "Exactly. You intend to, and you're great at it, but you've got 40 families and someone's always screaming about something. TailWag removes that friction. You're not adding another tool — you're replacing manual work."

**Then ask permission to follow up:**

> "I'm still beta testing this with a couple of daycares in the area. Would you be open to trying it free for two weeks to see if it actually saves you time?"

### If They Seem Hesitant ("We're fine as we are" or "I don't want more software")

Don't push. Validate them:

> "I get it — you're doing great, and the last thing you need is another login. Totally fair. I'm not here to sell you something complicated. I'm here because I see that your Google reviews are [specific observation] and I thought TailWag might fill a gap."

Then offer a smaller commitment:

> "Tell you what — even if you're not interested right now, let me leave you my number. If you ever get frustrated with no-shows or wish more parents left reviews, send me a text. No pressure. You know where to find me."

**Why this works:** You've given them permission to say no. You've respected their skepticism. And you've planted a seed. They'll think about you later — and if they regret saying no, reaching back out is easy.

## What to Bring: The Essentials

### 1. **Your Phone (Fully Charged)**
- Bring your charger or a power bank. A dead battery mid-demo kills credibility.
- Have your demo pre-loaded and tested.
- Make sure your WiFi is on and backup mobile data is available (so you're not dependent on their WiFi).

### 2. **A One-Page PDF Leave-Behind** (Print 5–10 copies)

Print this on cardstock or nice paper to feel premium. Title: "What TailWag Does (In 60 Seconds)"

Content:
```
TAILWAG: AI-Powered SMS for Independent Daycares

Three texts, automatically sent. Zero extra work from you.

1. REPORT CARDS
   AI-generated end-of-day texts that make parents smile.
   They know what their dog did, you don't have to write it.

2. REMINDERS
   Appointment reminders that reduce no-shows.
   One prevented no-show = one month free.

3. REVIEW REQUESTS
   Timed asks that turn happy clients into 5-star reviews.

SETUP: 5 minutes.
ONGOING: Runs in the background.
RESULT: Happy parents, more reviews, fewer no-shows.

Ready to try it? Text Summer at [your number]
```

Put your name, phone number, and email on the bottom. Make it look polished — this is the last thing they see before you leave.

### 3. **Your Phone Number on a Business Card** (Or a handwritten note if you don't have cards yet)

If you don't have formal business cards, a clean 3x5 card with your name, phone, email, and "TailWag - SMS for Independent Daycares" is professional enough. Handwritten is fine — it shows warmth.

### 4. **A Small Notebook** (For research)

You're going to see things:
- How many dogs they have on their Instagram
- What their biggest pain point is (ask directly: "What's the most annoying part of your day right now?")
- Competitors' daycares in the area (check their online reviews while you're there)
- Specific details about their business model (do they do boarding? Group play? Training?)

Take notes. You'll use these in follow-up emails.

### 5. **A Backup Plan: A Phone Screenshot or PDF**

If you're worried about tech failure, have a PDF of 2–3 demo screenshots on your phone so you can show them visually even if the live app isn't accessible.

## How to Handle Objections

### Objection 1: "We're already using [Gingr / DaySmart Pet / Other Daycare Software]"

**Don't say:** "Yeah, but their texts are generic..."

**Do say:** "Great — so you know how valuable a good messaging system is. The difference is, TailWag is designed specifically for the personalization that makes parents light up. Those generic reminder texts? Ours feel like they're coming from you because they mention what the dog actually did that day. Do you find that [platform]'s messaging does that?"

Most will say no. Then:

> "That's the gap I'm filling. You keep your [platform] for scheduling — TailWag just handles the texts that make people fall in love with you."

---

### Objection 2: "That's cool, but I don't have time to set it up"

**Don't say:** "It only takes 5 minutes!"

**Do say:** "I hear you. That's why I'm offering to set it up for you completely free, no commitment. I'll handle it. You'll get the first week of report cards and see if it's worth your time. If you hate it, we're done."

Remove the friction. Show them the payoff before asking them to do anything.

---

### Objection 3: "How much does it cost?"

**Don't mention price yet.** Instead, ask:

> "Great question. Before we talk cost, let me ask: If this prevented just one no-show a month, what's that worth to you?"

Wait for their answer. Then:

> "Okay, so if we're saving you $50–100 in revenue per month, and it takes zero effort on your part, price becomes pretty easy. What's the biggest bottleneck for you right now — no-shows, reviews, or just keeping parents in the loop?"

**Once you've identified their main pain point, THEN you talk pricing in terms of ROI:**

> "Our most popular plan is $49 a month. One prevented no-show covers that. One new 5-star review from better communication? That's pure bonus."

---

### Objection 4: "I'd have to ask the owner / my manager"

**Don't let this kill momentum.** Instead:

> "Smart. Who should I send more info to? I'll get you a summary of what we talked about plus a link to try it free, and they can check it out at their own pace. No pressure."

Then follow up 2 days later with an email (not a text) that includes:
- A brief recap of the conversation
- A link to a demo video or live demo environment
- The one-page leave-behind
- Your availability to answer questions

---

### Objection 5: "I'm not tech-savvy / I don't want something complicated"

**This is actually good news — it means they value simplicity.**

> "Honestly, that's exactly who TailWag is built for. No tech skills required. The setup is literally: import your client list, customize the welcome message, and turn it on. From there, the AI runs it. You don't have to log in or do anything. Does that feel doable?"

If they still hesitate:

> "Tell you what — I'll literally set it up for you on an Zoom call in the next few days. You watch, you learn, and you're live. No risk to you."

## Timing & Logistics

### Before You Show Up

1. **Research them** (15 minutes)
   - Google their business
   - Look at their Google reviews (count and sentiment)
   - Check their Instagram (how many dogs do they post? Do they engage with comments?)
   - Look for obvious pain points (lots of complaints about communication, few reviews, etc.)

2. **Create a personalized demo** (10 minutes)
   - Use dog names from their Instagram or recent reviews
   - Use their actual daycare name
   - Make it feel like it's already theirs

3. **Charge your phone and bring your charger**

4. **Print your one-page leave-behinds and business cards**

### During the Visit

- **Aim for 2–5 minutes if they're busy.** Respect that. An owner who feels rushed will resent the pitch.
- **If they're free and engaged, you have 10–15 minutes max.** Don't overstay.
- **Call them by name at least twice.** "Thanks for taking the time, [Name]." "I'll follow up with you, [Name]."
- **Read the room.** If they're on their phone or clearly distracted, cut the demo short and leave the one-pager.

### After You Leave (Critical)

Same-day, send a text (not email):

> "Hey [Name], thanks for the few minutes today. I sent you a demo link to your email [link]. No pressure, but if you have 2 minutes to try it, I'd love your thoughts. You know where to find me."

This does two things:
1. It confirms you got their number (builds trust).
2. It reminds them to actually look at what you showed them.

## The Mindset

You're not trying to make a sale on Tuesday. You're trying to start a conversation that leads to a trial that leads to a paying customer. A walk-in demo is successful if:

1. You leave a good impression (warm, knowledgeable, respectful of their time)
2. They remember TailWag and what it does
3. They know how to reach you
4. They try the free trial or watch the demo video

That's it. Everything else is follow-up.

Good luck — you've got this.
