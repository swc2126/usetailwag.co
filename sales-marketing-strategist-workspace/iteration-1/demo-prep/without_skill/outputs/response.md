# TailWag Walk-In Demo Prep Guide

## Your 30-Second Elevator Pitch

**Goal: Get permission to do a 5-10 minute demo**

"Hi, I'm Summer, the founder of TailWag. We've built an AI-powered texting platform specifically for dog daycares like yours. It automates the photos, updates, and confirmations you're currently doing manually—freeing up your team's time while keeping owners way more engaged. Do you have a quick minute to see how it works?"

---

## What to Bring

### Digital
- **Your phone** with TailWag already logged in and ready to demo
  - Pre-load 2-3 example conversations showing:
    - Automated photo messages with captions
    - A dog check-in confirmation flow
    - An owner inquiry response
  - Have screenshots/screen recordings queued up if live demo isn't possible
- **Backup**: Printed one-pager with your contact info, basic value prop, and a QR code linking to a demo video or landing page

### Physical
- **Business cards** (small stack of ~10-15)
  - Include: Your name, title, phone number, email, website
  - Back side: Quick value prop (e.g., "AI-powered SMS for dog daycares. Save time. Delight owners.")
- **Notepad & pen** for:
  - Taking notes on their pain points
  - Getting contact info if they're interested
  - Jotting down follow-up details

### Optional But Helpful
- **Tablet or second device** if your phone screen is small
- **Price sheet** (printed) if you've already set pricing
  - Keep it visible but don't lead with cost
- **Testimonials/case studies** if you have any early users
  - Even just 1-2 sentences from beta testers works

---

## Your Core Demo Flow (5-7 minutes)

### 1. The Problem (30 seconds)
"Right now, how do owners get updates about their dogs? And what does that process look like for your team?"

*Listen. Take notes. This is gold.*

### 2. The Solution (1-2 minutes)
"TailWag automates those updates via text. The system:
- **Captures moments** throughout the day (you or your staff snap a photo)
- **Generates captions** using AI (no copywriting needed)
- **Sends to owners** automatically or on your schedule
- **Handles responses** like 'pick-up questions' so staff doesn't get interrupted"

### 3. Live Walkthrough (2-3 minutes)
Show them:
- How a staff member would snap a photo + add a quick note (very simple input)
- How the AI generates a friendly caption
- How it gets sent to the dog owner via SMS
- How owner responses come back (and where staff sees them)

### 4. The Ask (30 seconds)
"Could I follow up with you next week? I'd love to set up a 15-minute call to talk more about your workflow and see if this could save your team time."

*Get their email or phone number before you leave.*

## Common Objections & Responses

### Objection #1: "We already have a system / app for this"
**Response:**
"I hear you. What's working well about [their current system], and what's the biggest pain point? A lot of daycares I've talked to have an app, but staff find texting faster than logging into another platform—especially when they're in the middle of a play session. Could TailWag complement what you already have, or handle a specific workflow that feels clunky now?"

*Goal: Find the gap in their current solution.*

---

### Objection #2: "Owners want photos in an app, not texts"
**Response:**
"That's fair. Some owners do prefer apps. But here's what we're seeing: **owners check their texts immediately** (90%+ open rate within 5 minutes), but they log into apps maybe once a day. Plus, texts feel more personal and real-time. You could do both—TailWag for daily quick updates, and your app for the official record. Does that feel like it could work for your owners?"

*Goal: Reframe texts as complementary, not replacement.*

---

### Objection #3: "This feels like it would replace our personal touch"
**Response:**
"Actually, it's the opposite. TailWag handles the busywork—so your team has more time to **actually be with the dogs** instead of writing captions. The AI generates a starting point, but you can always tweak it to match your voice. It's about giving your staff breathing room, not cutting them out."

*Goal: Position it as a time-saver that enables better service.*

---

### Objection #4: "How much does this cost?"
**Response:**
"Great question. We're still finalizing our exact pricing model, but we're positioning this as something that pays for itself by reducing admin time and increasing owner retention. I'd love to learn more about your team size and volume first—then we can talk numbers that make sense for you. Does that work?"

*Alternative (if you have pricing):*
"We're starting at [X] per month, and most daycares see ROI within the first month just from time savings. Can I send you a pricing sheet with a few package options?"

*Goal: Don't oversell on cost; focus on value first.*

---

### Objection #5: "We're not interested right now"
**Response:**
"I totally understand. Can I ask—is timing the issue, or is this just not a priority? I ask because sometimes daycares say 'not now,' but they're slammed in summer. If you just want me to check back in a couple months, that's totally cool. Or if there's a specific problem I could help solve, I'm here for it."

*Goal: Find out if it's "no forever" or "no now but yes later."*

---

### Objection #6: "Privacy/data security concerns"
**Response:**
"Smart question. We take data privacy seriously—[mention any certifications or security practices you have: encryption, GDPR compliance, no selling data, etc.]. I'm happy to send you our full security documentation or connect you with our [technical person] if you want to dig deeper. Is security the main concern, or are there other questions?"

*Note: Have your actual security/privacy policies documented and ready to share.*

---

### Objection #7: "I need to talk to my partner/owner/manager"
**Response:**
"Absolutely. Who should I loop in? Would it help if I sent over a quick demo video or one-pager they could review before we all talk? I want to make sure everyone's on the same page."

*Goal: Don't leave without a clear next step. Get the decision-maker's contact.*

## Before You Leave

**If They're Interested:**
- [ ] Get their name, title, phone, and email
- [ ] Suggest a specific follow-up day/time ("I can do a quick call next Tuesday at 2 PM, does that work?")
- [ ] Offer to send a 2-minute demo video or one-pager they can share with their team
- [ ] Ask for a referral: "If you know another daycare owner who might benefit, I'd love an intro"

**If They're Not Interested:**
- [ ] Still leave your card
- [ ] Ask for permission to check back in 3-6 months ("Things change, and I want to stay on your radar")
- [ ] Ask for a referral anyway

**After Every Demo (within 24 hours):**
- [ ] Send a follow-up email with: your demo video link, one-pager, your calendar link
- [ ] Personalize it: reference something they said ("I know you mentioned the photo uploads take forever—TailWag could handle that in seconds")
- [ ] Include a clear CTA ("Let's chat next week—pick a time here: [calendar link]")

## Mindset Going In

1. **You're not selling; you're problem-solving.** Listen more than you talk. Their pain is your best sales tool.

2. **Everyone's busy.** Respect their time. If they give you 5 minutes, give them a tight, punchy demo—don't ramble.

3. **Confidence is attractive.** You know your product solves a real problem. Own that.

4. **Objections are opportunities.** They're not rejections; they're clarifications. Lean into them.

5. **It's a numbers game.** Not every daycare will be a fit. That's okay. Focus on the ones that are.

6. **Your goal isn't a sale today; it's a conversation next week.** Walk-in demos are seed-planting, not closing.

---

## Quick Confidence Boosters

- **You're solving a real problem.** Daycares HATE manual photo updates and owner communication friction.
- **You're the expert.** You built this. You know it better than anyone.
- **They'll remember you showed up in person.** Most competitors don't. That's a competitive advantage.
- **Even a "no" is valuable data.** Use it to sharpen your pitch.
