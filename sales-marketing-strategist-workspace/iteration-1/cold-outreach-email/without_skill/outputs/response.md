# Cold Outreach Email for Daycare Owners

## Subject Line Option 1
Subject: Quick question about your daycare operations

## Subject Line Option 2
Subject: How [Daycare Name] could save time on client updates

---

## Email Body

Hi [Owner/Manager Name],

I came across [Daycare Name] while researching dog daycares in the [City/Area] area, and I was impressed by your setup.

I'm reaching out because I'm working on something that might save you time—it's an SMS platform built specifically for dog daycares to send photo updates and care summaries to dog parents automatically.

A lot of daycare owners tell us that sending individual updates throughout the day is time-consuming, especially when they're managing multiple dogs. We're building TailWag to handle that in minutes instead of hours.

I'm still in early stages, and I'm talking to a few daycare owners in Texas to understand what would actually be helpful. Would you have 15 minutes for a quick call or email chat about how you currently handle owner updates?

No pressure either way—I just want to make sure we're building something that actually solves a real problem for you.

Thanks for considering,
Summer
TailWag
Irving, TX

---

## Email Variants & Tips

### Variant A: Location-Specific (More Personal)
Replace the generic opening with something like:
"I came across your daycare on Google and noticed you're in Irving/[nearby area]. Since I'm based here too, I thought I'd reach out..."

### Variant B: Problem-First (Stronger Hook)
"Hi [Name], I've been researching dog daycares in Texas, and I keep hearing the same challenge: owners expect photo updates during the day, but sending them individually eats up your team's time. I'm building a tool to fix that. Would you be open to a quick chat about how this might work for your business?"

---

## Pre-Send Checklist

- [ ] Personalize the name and daycare name (not generic "Hi there")
- [ ] Research if they have a website or social media (mention something specific if possible)
- [ ] Keep the email short (this is 150-180 words—scannable)
- [ ] Include a clear ask (15-min call or email follow-up)
- [ ] Make it easy to respond (just a "yes" or "let's chat")
- [ ] Send from a professional email address with your name
- [ ] Consider sending Tuesday-Thursday 9am-12pm (best open rates)

---

## Follow-Up Strategy

**If no response after 5 days:** Send one follow-up email
"Hey [Name], just wanted to make sure this reached you. No rush—just thought TailWag might be useful. Let me know if you want to chat."

**If they respond interested:** Offer a 15-20 minute call over Zoom or phone—focus on listening to their current workflow before pitching.

---

## Customization Notes for Summer

- Replace [Daycare Name], [Owner/Manager Name], [City/Area] with actual info from your Google search
- If you find their social media or website, you can reference something specific ("I saw you post about your new play area" or "Your Google reviews mention great owner communication")
- Adjust tone if needed—this is friendly but professional; feel free to be warmer if that fits your brand
- Consider your email signature (include phone number for easy callback)
