# TailWag Go-to-Market Plan: First 10 Customers

## Executive Summary
Your path to the first 10 customers should focus on **high-touch, personal outreach** to local and regional dog daycares in the Irving, TX area and beyond. At this stage, you're validating product-market fit and gathering testimonials—not scaling. Target boutique daycares ($500K-$5M revenue) that already send some communication to owners but lack automation.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

### 1.1 Define Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
**Who to target:**
- Dog daycares with 30-100+ active dogs (large enough to care about efficiency, small enough to be personal)
- Owners/managers who manually write report cards or use spreadsheets/email
- Located in Texas initially, expandable to nearby states
- Positive Google reviews indicate they care about reputation (ideal for review request feature)

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Facilities using enterprise software (PetSmart, Rover, etc.) already integrated with email
- Very small operations (<20 dogs) that may not see ROI
- Facilities that don't currently communicate progress to owners

### 1.2 List Building
**Action items:**
1. **Local search:** Google Maps search for "dog daycare Irving TX," "dog boarding near me," etc.
   - Screenshot names, addresses, phone numbers, and Google review links
   - Build a spreadsheet with 50+ prospects in Texas (focus on Irving, Dallas, Austin, Houston)

2. **Regional expansion:** Extend to Oklahoma, Arkansas, Louisiana (1-2 hour radius)

3. **Online directories:** Check Yelp, BringFido, Care.com

4. **Social proof hunting:** Look for daycares with strong Google/Yelp reviews but outdated websites or Facebook pages (sign they manage communication manually)

**Target list size:** 50-100 qualified prospects for cold outreach

### 1.3 Craft Your Elevator Pitch
**Problem statement you'll use:**
- "Managing dog daycare updates is time-consuming. Owners want regular updates. You want them to rebook and leave reviews."

**Value prop (one-liner):**
- "TailWag automates personalized daily report cards, appointment reminders, and Google review requests—freeing you to focus on the dogs."

**Proof points to mention (as you have them):**
- Saves 5-10 hours/week on manual updates
- Increases rebooking rates (parent engagement drives loyalty)
- Automatically requests Google reviews (critical for daycares)

## Phase 2: Multi-Channel Outreach (Weeks 3-4)

### 2.1 Phone Cold Calls (Highest Touch)
**Why it works:** Daycares are small teams; a warm voice beats an email.

**Script framework:**
```
"Hi [Manager/Owner name], this is Summer with TailWag. I'm building software
specifically for dog daycares to automate update text messages and appointment
reminders. Do you currently send daily updates to owners?
[Listen] I think I can save you hours each week. Do you have 15 minutes next
week for a quick walk-through?"
```

**Execution:**
- Call during non-peak hours (9-10 AM, 3-4 PM—not lunch/pickup times)
- Aim for 5-10 calls per day
- Track responses in a spreadsheet
- Target: 30% positive response rate (3 per 10 calls)

### 2.2 Email Outreach (Warm Follow-Up)
**Timing:** Send 24-48 hours after a positive phone call.

**Email framework:**
```
Subject: Quick demo for [Daycare Name]—save 5+ hours/week on updates

Hi [Name],

Great talking with you yesterday. As promised, here's how TailWag works:

1. Owner books appointment in your calendar
2. TailWag automatically sends personalized daily updates via SMS
3. After visit, automated request for Google review

This saves time and increases rebooking rates.

Would [Tuesday/Thursday] afternoon work for a 15-min call to see if it's a fit?

—Summer
TailWag
```

**Key elements:**
- Short, benefit-focused
- Include a one-minute video demo link (Loom)
- Time-specific CTA
- Personal (from you, not generic)

### 2.3 LinkedIn Outreach (Bonus)
**For prospects you can't call:**
- Find daycare owner/manager on LinkedIn
- Personal message: "I noticed [Daycare Name] has great reviews on Google. I built software to help daycares automate owner updates and review requests. Curious if you'd be open to a 15-min call?"
- Lower response rate (~5-10%), but adds touchpoints

### 2.4 Facebook Groups & Communities
**Join and participate:**
- Local Irving business groups
- Texas dog daycare owner groups
- Dog industry forums (Reddit's r/dogs, BringFido community)
- Share insight, not sales pitch
- When relevant: "We're building TailWag for daycare efficiency—happy to chat if anyone's interested"

## Phase 3: Sales Conversations (Weeks 3-5)

### 3.1 Discovery Call Structure (15-20 min)
**Your goals:**
1. Understand their current process (how do they send updates? How much time?)
2. Identify pain points (manually writing cards? Low engagement?)
3. Gauge willingness to try (are they open to new tools?)
4. Gather product feedback (what features matter most?)

**Script outline:**
```
Small talk: "How long have you been running [Daycare]?"

Problem diagnosis:
- How do you currently send updates to owners?
- How much time does that take per day?
- What's the biggest pain in your process?

Solution fit:
- I built TailWag to [solve their stated problem].
- Would you be interested in trying it for [X days] to see if it works for you?

Next steps:
- If yes: "Great. Let's set up a demo. I can show you in 10 minutes how it'd work for your schedule."
- If no: "No problem. Can I check back in 2 months?"
```

### 3.2 Demo Strategy
**Keep it short and specific:**
- Show how it integrates with their calendar
- Customize one example with their daycare name
- Show SMS preview of a report card
- Show the Google review request workflow
- Ask: "Does this save you time vs. what you do today?"

**Do NOT:**
- Over-sell features
- Discuss pricing yet (at first 5 customers, consider free or heavily discounted pilot)
- Get bogged down in technical details

## Phase 4: Conversion & Pilots (Weeks 4-6)

### 4.1 Pilot Program Strategy
**For your first 5 customers, consider:**
- Free 30-day pilot (you need testimonials and product feedback)
- After 30 days: "$99/month or equivalent value trade" (cash or testimonial/case study)
- Clear success metrics: "By day 30, we'll measure time saved, owner engagement, and Google review impact"

**Why this works:**
- Low risk for them to try
- You get real-world usage data
- They become advocates if they see value

### 4.2 Onboarding
**Keep it simple:**
- 15-min call to set up SMS integration
- Show them the admin dashboard
- Send them a resource guide (PDF or Loom video)
- Check in after 5 days: "How's it going? Any questions?"

### 4.3 Weekly Check-Ins
- Text or call weekly during the pilot
- Ask: "How many owners have gotten updates? Feedback?"
- Be ready to tweak features based on feedback
- Document wins (e.g., "Owner rebooked because of daily updates")

## Phase 5: Scaling to 10 (Weeks 5-8)

### 5.1 Referral Program
**Once you have 2-3 happy customers:**
- Offer them a reward (discount, cash, or free month) for each referral
- "If you know another daycare owner who'd benefit, I'll give you [reward]"
- Daycares talk to each other (training events, supply vendors, etc.)

### 5.2 Case Study Leverage
**Document early wins:**
- Ask first 3 customers: "Can I share your story as a case study?"
- Create one-page case study: Daycare name → Problem → TailWag solution → Results (time saved, reviews, rebooking)
- Use in emails to prospects: "Similar daycare saved 6 hours/week..."

### 5.3 Local Events & Partnerships
- **Dog industry events:** Pet expos, dog trainer meetups, local business mixers
- **Partnerships:** Partner with pet supply vendors, dog trainers, grooming facilities that refer clients
- **Chamber of Commerce:** Join Irving Chamber, attend networking events

## Key Success Factors

### 1. **Speed to First Customer**
- Aim to sign your first pilot within 2 weeks of launch
- Urgency: Early customers = product feedback + testimonials

### 2. **Personal Touch Wins**
- You're competing against: "We'll think about it" and inertia
- A personal call from the founder beats a cold email
- Build relationships (they're small business owners like you)

### 3. **Listen More Than You Sell**
- During discovery calls, spend 70% listening, 30% talking
- Take detailed notes on pain points—these become your messaging
- Adapt your pitch based on what you hear

### 4. **Pilot Success = Word of Mouth**
- First 5 customers are your sales team
- If they love TailWag, they'll tell other daycare owners
- Invest time in making them successful

### 5. **Track Everything**
- Spreadsheet with prospect name, phone, email, call date, response, outcome
- Note objections (e.g., "worried about tech integration")—these inform product roadmap
- Track time spent—find what's most effective

## Common Objections & Responses

## Red Flags & Pivot Points

**If after Week 4 you have:**
- 0 positive responses to 30+ cold calls → **Pivot:** Maybe the messaging isn't resonating. Re-interview the daycares you reached out to and ask: "Why weren't you interested?"
- 3+ discovery calls but 0 pilots → **Pivot:** Pricing may be too high, or the product doesn't solve a painful problem. Ask pilots directly: "What would make you say yes?"
- Low engagement during pilots → **Pivot:** The onboarding may be confusing or the feature set needs tweaking. Get on calls with pilots weekly.
