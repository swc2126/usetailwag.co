Before Your First Day

Your TailWag specialist will handle all the technical configuration. Your job is to gather the information below before your Day 1 setup call or visit. The more prepared you are, the faster you'll be live.

⏱ Time to complete

Most business owners finish this checklist in 30–45 minutes. You can do it in stages — you don't need everything at once, but having it all ready makes Day 1 much smoother.

2.1 Information to Gather

Your Google Business Profile

You need a verified Google Business Profile for the review feature to work. If you don't have one, see Section 6. Your TailWag specialist will find your Google Place ID.

Your booking software

What do you use to manage appointments? Gingr, Time to Pet, Boulevard, Vagaro, Square, or paper — just let your TailWag specialist know.

Your client list

Current active clients with: pet name, owner first name, owner cell phone. A spreadsheet, printed list, or reading them aloud all work fine.

Your business phone number

The number TailWag messages will appear to come from — your main business line or a dedicated number Your TailWag specialist sets up for you.

Staff who will use TailWag

First name and role of each staff member who will input daily notes. Have them available for a brief 10-minute orientation on Day 1.

2.2 Pre-Setup Checklist

Check off each item before your Day 1 session:

  • I have a Google Business Profile set up for my daycare. (If not — business.google.com, free, ~15 min)
  • I know what booking software I use (or that I use a paper system)
  • I have a client list with pet name, owner name, and cell phone
  • I have confirmed clients have agreed to receive texts — see 2.3 below
  • I know which staff members will enter daily notes
  • I have a device (phone, tablet, or computer) for staff to access the portal

2.3 SMS Consent — What You Need to Know

⚠ This step is required

Before TailWag sends any text message to a pet owner, that owner must have agreed to receive text messages from your business. This is a legal requirement under federal law (TCPA). Do not skip this step. Most daycares already have this covered through their enrollment process.

Your SituationWhat to Do
My enrollment form already asks clients to agree to texts Great — you're covered. Bring your enrollment form to Day 1 for your TailWag specialist to confirm the language is sufficient.
My enrollment form doesn't mention texts Add a simple opt-in checkbox before going live. Your TailWag specialist will provide suggested language to add to your form.
I'm not sure if I have consent for existing clients Your TailWag specialist will help you send a one-time re-consent message to your existing client list before going live. Takes about 10 minutes.
I'm enrolling new clients after TailWag is live Add the opt-in language to your enrollment form before their first visit. Every new consenting client is automatically eligible.

Suggested opt-in language for your enrollment form

"By providing your mobile phone number, you agree to receive automated text messages from [Your Business Name] regarding your pet's visits, appointment reminders, and follow-up communications. Message and data rates may apply. You may opt out at any time by replying STOP."

Section 4

Daily Staff Operations

What your team does every day — and what TailWag handles automatically. This section is written for your staff. Feel free to print it and post it near your computer or tablet. The entire daily process takes about 30 seconds per dog.

The core principle: Your staff provides the detail. TailWag provides the words.

TailWag Daily Staff Checklist

During or after each dog's visit

Open the TailWag portal on your device.
Find the dog's name in today's check-in list.
Type 3–5 words describing their day in the Notes field.
If it was a great day, check the 'Great Day' box.
Click Save. That's it.

By end of business each day

Make sure every dog checked in today has a note entered.
Any dog marked "Great Day" will automatically receive a review follow-up.

For no-shows

Mark any dog that was expected but didn't appear as a no-show so it's captured in the morning summary.

If a client asks to stop receiving texts

Find them in the portal and mark them as opted out — or they can reply STOP to any message and TailWag handles it automatically.

4.2 Writing Good Notes

The better the notes, the more personal the message feels. Notes don't need to be sentences — just a few specific words are enough.

❌ Notes to Avoid✓ Notes That Work Great
"Good day""played fetch, tired out, ate well"
"Normal""made a new friend, very social today"
"Fine""shy at first, warmed up fast, napped after lunch"
"OK""high energy, ran laps, couldn't stop zooming"
"Same as usual""gentle as always, cuddled with staff, great listener"

Tip for staff

Think of the notes as what you'd tell the owner if you had 10 seconds at pickup. Even one specific detail — "finally played with the big dogs" or "discovered she loves the sprinklers" — makes the message feel completely personal.

5.3 Handling Client Feedback

If a client replies to a TailWag message with a concern or question, their message lands in your Inbox tab in the portal — flagged for your attention. You're seeing it before it can become a public review.

1
Read the feedback without reacting immediately
Take a breath before responding. Sometimes negative feedback is a misunderstanding. Read it once to understand what happened.
2
Respond within 24 hours
Reach out to the owner directly by phone or text. A simple "Thank you for letting me know — can we talk about what happened?" goes a long way.
3
Resolve the issue
Offer whatever is appropriate — a conversation, a discount on the next visit, or an explanation. Most clients who feel heard do not escalate.
4
Note it internally
If a pattern emerges — same dog, same staff member, same time of day — that's valuable operational feedback. TailWag is giving you data you wouldn't have had otherwise.

Why the Inbox matters: Every client message that comes to your Inbox is a chance to respond before anything escalates. Most businesses only find out something went wrong when they see the one-star review. TailWag surfaces these moments while you can still do something about them.
