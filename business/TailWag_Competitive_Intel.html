THE MARKET

Market Snapshot

The U.S. pet daycare market is valued at $1.87 billion in 2025, growing at 8.78% CAGR to $2.85B by 2030.[1] Day boarding represents roughly 67% of the market.[1] 71% of U.S. households own pets — 51% own dogs.[2]

The pet daycare software market is a separate $1B+ space growing 8–12.5% CAGR[3] — but it's wildly fragmented with no vendor commanding more than mid-single-digit market share.[4] An estimated 30–50% of independent daycares still operate with no software at all — using binders, spreadsheets, and Google Docs.[5]

PET PARENT EXPECTATIONS

What Today's Pet Parents Demand

Pet parents in 2026 expect the same digital transparency from their daycare that they get from food delivery and ride-share apps. The gap between expectation and reality is massive — and it's where TailWag lives.

87% want useful reminders[7]

For appointments, vaccinations, and scheduling — yet 42% say they don't receive automated reminders for essential care.

77% prefer text over phone[7]

Pet parents want text/chat communication — they don't want to call. SMS is the native channel for this buyer.

83% want health record access[7]

Digital transparency isn't optional anymore. Parents expect to see their pet's info, activity, and visit history.

31–40% will switch providers[7]

If the digital experience is poor. Under-35 pet parents are especially willing to leave for a better-communicating daycare.

COMPETITOR LANDSCAPE

Two Categories of Competition

TailWag competes with two very different types of platforms. Understanding the distinction is critical to positioning.

Category 1

Pet Daycare Management Platforms

Full-stack operations software for scheduling, booking, payments, and client management. Messaging is a feature buried inside a bigger system.

Players: Gingr, PetExec, DaySmart Pet, ProPet, Pawfinity, Time To Pet, Paw Partner, Revelation Pets

Category 2

Generic Business SMS / Reputation Tools

Horizontal platforms that serve every industry — restaurants, dental, auto repair. Not built for pet care. Expensive and overcomplicated for a 10-person daycare.

Players: Podium, Birdeye, Weave, Broadly

The Consolidation Story You Should Know

Togetherwork now owns PetExec, Gingr, and Revelation Pets — giving them the largest combined footprint (~9,000 facilities). But they're forcing PetExec users onto Gingr with a punitive 1% payment processing fee[9], creating a wave of frustrated daycare owners actively looking for alternatives. That migration pain is a direct sales opportunity for TailWag — these owners are already primed to listen to something new.

What "Fragmented" Means for TailWag

No platform has more than ~6% of the market. This means there's no incumbent gorilla to unseat. Daycare owners aren't locked into ecosystems the way businesses are with Salesforce or HubSpot. Switching costs are low, loyalty is thin, and the door is wide open for a focused challenger that does one thing better than everyone else.

GREEN SPACE

The Gaps Nobody Is Filling

After scouting every competitor, here's where the market is wide open for TailWag.

Gap 1

AI-Personalized Communication

Every platform either sends generic template texts or requires manual writing. Nobody uses AI to generate personalized, per-dog, per-visit messages automatically. This is TailWag's entire product — and it doesn't exist anywhere else.

Gap 2

SMS as Core, Not Add-On

Gingr charges extra for 2-way SMS. PetExec charges $19.99/mo. DaySmart limits you to 500 texts at the base tier. SMS is an afterthought on every platform. TailWag is SMS-first — it's the whole point.

Gap 3

Integrated Review Generation

Every pet platform outsources reviews to Broadly or Birdeye (add-on cost, separate dashboard). TailWag builds timed review requests natively into the SMS flow — no third-party tool, no extra cost.

Gap 4

The "No Software" Market

~40% of independent daycares use no software at all. Full management platforms are intimidating and expensive. TailWag's "do one thing brilliantly" approach is the perfect first step — low friction, immediate ROI, no migration headache.

Gap 5

Affordable Communication Layer

Daycares on Gingr or PetExec who want better communication are stuck paying for Broadly or Birdeye on top of their management platform. TailWag can sit alongside their existing system as a communication upgrade — at a fraction of the cost of Podium.

Gap 6

Report Cards That Write Themselves

Gingr, PetExec, ProPet all require staff to manually write report cards. With 20–80 dogs per day, that means most daycares just... don't do it. TailWag's AI automates what everyone wishes they could do but can't sustain manually.

RECOMMENDATION 2

Unique Value Props — Two Audiences

TailWag's pitch changes depending on whether the daycare has an existing system or nothing at all. Here's the playbook for each.

Audience A

Daycares with No System

~40% of the market. Using binders, spreadsheets, memory.

Lead Message

"You don't need a $150/month management platform to start wowing your clients. TailWag does three things — report cards, reminders, and reviews — and it does them better than anyone. Five-minute setup. Runs in the background. You'll see results in week one."

Key Angles

  • Low friction, high impact — no migration, no learning curve, no complex onboarding. Plug in and go.
  • The "first step" positioning — "You don't need to overhaul your whole business. Start with the thing that makes clients stay and reviews grow."
  • Affordability — $99/mo Starter tier is less than a Gingr subscription, and they get AI-powered communication that Gingr doesn't even offer.
  • ROI in dollars — "One prevented no-show pays for TailWag. One new 5-star review is worth $200–500 in new business."
  • Do what you can't do manually — "You mean to text every parent every day. But with 30 dogs, you just can't. TailWag can."

Objection Flip

"I don't need more software." → "That's exactly why TailWag works — it's not a management platform. It's three automations that run in the background. You'll forget it's there, but your clients won't."

Audience B

Daycares on Gingr, PetExec, DaySmart, etc.

~60% of digitized daycares. Already paying $50–$155/mo for ops software.

Lead Message

"You've got scheduling and payments handled. But how many personalized texts did you send to pet parents last week? TailWag plugs into what you already have and handles the communication piece that your management platform was never built to do."

Key Angles

  • Companion, not replacement — "Keep Gingr for booking. Add TailWag for the part Gingr can't do — AI-personalized daily report cards."
  • Cheaper than their current add-ons — Gingr + Broadly + SMS add-on costs $180+. TailWag at $179 replaces both add-ons AND does it better.
  • What their platform can't do — "Gingr's report cards require staff to write every note. With 40 dogs, how many actually get sent? TailWag sends all 40, every day, personalized, automatically."
  • The AI gap — Not a single pet daycare platform has AI-generated messaging. "Your current software was built in 2018. TailWag was built in 2026."
  • Review generation without a third party — "Why pay Broadly $X/month when TailWag sends timed review requests as part of the flow?"

Objection Flip

"I already have Gingr/PetExec." → "Great — that means you've got operations covered. TailWag handles the one thing they don't: making every pet parent feel like their dog got VIP treatment, every single day."

Timed to land when pet parents are happiest — right after reading a great report card. This is the one-two punch: delight them, then ask for the review. Competitors outsource this to Broadly or Birdeye at extra cost.

ROI Driver

Five minutes. No migration. No integrations needed. No training. This is the anti-Gingr, anti-Podium pitch. "You don't need to change anything about how you run your daycare. Just add TailWag."

Feature Roadmap — What to Build and When

Based on competitor gaps, market demand, and what will drive the most revenue at each stage:

NOW — April/May 2026

Lock In the Core

Ship what you're selling. The three automations (report cards, reminders, review requests) need to work flawlessly before adding anything else. Focus on reliability and onboarding speed.

  • Onboarding flow — Self-serve setup that takes under 5 minutes. The #1 complaint about competitors is onboarding complexity.
  • Client dashboard — Simple view showing texts sent, reviews generated, no-shows prevented. Proof of value that prevents churn.
  • Photo attachment support — Let staff snap a photo that gets included in the AI report card. Gingr does this manually; TailWag should do it with one tap.
Q3 2026 — July/Aug/Sept

Expand the Communication Layer

Once you have 20–30 clients and know what they love, build the features that increase stickiness and justify the Growth/Partner tiers.

  • 2-way SMS conversations — Let pet parents reply to report cards and staff respond from a dashboard. PetExec charges $19.99/mo extra for this. Include it in Growth tier.
  • Vaccination/appointment auto-reminders — "Your pup is due for a Bordetella update" texts. Solves a huge compliance headache. 87% of pet parents want this.
  • Birthday & milestone messages — "Happy 3rd birthday, Cooper! 🐾" Auto-triggered. Costs nothing to send, builds incredible loyalty. No competitor does this.
  • Monthly performance email to owner — "This month: 847 report cards sent, 12 new Google reviews, 6 no-shows prevented, est. $2,100 in retained revenue." This is the email that prevents cancellation.
Q4 2026 — Oct/Nov/Dec

Intelligence & Integration

With a growing customer base, add features that make TailWag irreplaceable and open the door to higher-tier pricing.

  • Gingr / PetExec / DaySmart integrations — Pull booking and pet data automatically. This removes the "I already have a system" objection entirely. TailWag becomes the communication layer that plugs into whatever they use.
  • Churn detection — AI flags clients whose visit frequency is declining. Auto-trigger a "We miss [dog name]!" text. This is the "silent leaver" problem solved. No competitor touches this.
  • Review response AI — Auto-draft responses to Google reviews (owner approves before posting). Podium charges $599/mo for this. Include it in Partner tier.
  • Multi-location dashboard — For the Partner tier. See all locations in one view. Positions TailWag for scaling with multi-unit operators.
2027 — EXPANSION PLAYS

Platform Growth

These are the features that turn TailWag from a communication tool into a growth platform for daycares — and potentially expand into adjacent verticals.

  • Pet parent mobile app — Branded to the daycare. Report card history, booking, payments. This is the "Gingr replacement" play.
  • Loyalty & referral program — "Refer a friend, get a free day" — managed through SMS. Turns pet parents into sales reps.
  • Waitlist management — AI-managed waitlists that auto-text when a spot opens. Solves a major booking gap.
  • Vertical expansion — Groomers, boarders, dog walkers, vet clinics. Same AI communication engine, different templates. But don't do this until daycare is locked down.
