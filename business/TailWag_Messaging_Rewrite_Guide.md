# TailWag Messaging Rewrite Guide
## Outcome-First Positioning — Remove AI as the Headline

---

### The Principle

Lead with what the daycare owner **gets**. Reveal how it works (AI) deeper in the experience. AI is the engine, not the pitch.

---

## Website Rewrites

### Page Title (browser tab)

**Before:** TailWag — AI-Powered SMS for Independent Daycares
**After:** TailWag — Personalized SMS for Independent Daycares

---

### Hero Section Kicker (small uppercase label above headline)

**Before:** AI-POWERED CLIENT COMMUNICATION
**After:** SMART CLIENT COMMUNICATION

---

### Hero Headline

**Before:** (implied from current tagline) AI-Powered SMS for Independent Daycares

**After options — pick one:**

1. "Every visit, a story your clients can't forget."
   *(This is already on the site as the hero headline and it is great. Keep it.)*

2. "Personalized report cards, fewer no-shows, more 5-star reviews. All on autopilot."
   *(Alternative if you want to lead with the three outcomes.)*

**Recommendation:** Keep #1. It is outcome-first, emotionally resonant, and already working. The kicker change above is all you need in the hero.

---

### The System — Step 1 (Report Card)

**Before:** "AI writes a personalized message from staff notes. Sent same day."
**After:** "Your staff notes become a warm, personalized message. Sent same day."

*Why: The staff notes are the input. The personalized message is the output. The reader does not need to know AI is in the middle — they need to know it happens automatically from what the staff already does.*

---

### FAQ — Question #1

**Before:** "How does the AI write personalized report cards?"
**After:** "How does TailWag personalize every report card?"

*Why: Same question, but now the subject is TailWag (the product they are evaluating) not "the AI" (a technology they did not ask about).*

---

### FAQ — Answer #1

**Before:** "You and your staff log brief notes during or after each visit (e.g., 'Buddy was social, played fetch, napped 2-3pm'). TailWag's AI takes those raw notes and expands them into warm, detailed messages that sound like your team — without requiring extra work from you."

**After:** "You and your staff log brief notes during or after each visit — 'Buddy was social, played fetch, napped 2-3pm.' TailWag takes those notes and turns them into warm, detailed messages that sound like your team wrote them. No extra work from you."

*Why: Removed "AI" entirely. The answer still explains the mechanic clearly. "TailWag takes those notes and turns them into" is accurate and product-focused. If someone wants to know the technology behind it, they will ask — and that is a great conversation to have in a demo, not on a landing page.*

---

### Where AI Should Still Appear

These are the places where "AI" earns its spot — as a supporting detail, not a headline:

**How It Works section** (deeper on the page):
Add a single line like: "Powered by AI that understands what a great daycare day sounds like — so every message feels personal, not automated."

**FAQ** (if someone asks directly):
If you want, you can add a new FAQ: "Does TailWag use AI?" with an answer like: "Yes. TailWag uses AI to turn your staff's daily notes into personalized messages that sound like your team — not like software. It is the reason we can personalize a message for every dog, every visit, without adding work to your day."

**In demos and sales conversations:**
This is where AI becomes a "wow" moment. Show the staff notes going in, the personalized message coming out. Let them see the magic. In person, AI is a feature. On a landing page, it is jargon.

## Collateral Updates

### Founders Circle One-Pager

**Before (Essentials feature):** "AI-powered report cards after every visit"
**After:** "Personalized report cards after every visit"

### Service Agreement

**Before (subtitle):** "TailWag — AI-Powered SMS for Independent Daycares"
**After:** "TailWag — Personalized SMS for Independent Daycares"

**Before (Section 1):** "TailWag provides the Subscriber with access to its AI-powered SMS communication platform"
**After:** "TailWag provides the Subscriber with access to its SMS communication platform"

*Note: Keep "AI-generated messages" in Section 8 (Limitation of Liability) — that is a legal context where specificity about the technology matters for liability purposes.*

### Pitch Scripts / Outreach Emails

Replace any instance of "AI-powered" with outcome language:
- "AI-powered report cards" → "personalized report cards"
- "AI-powered SMS platform" → "SMS platform that personalizes every message"
- "uses AI to..." → "automatically turns your staff notes into..."

### Email Subject Lines

Never lead with AI. Lead with the result:
- ~~"AI-Powered SMS for Your Daycare"~~ → "What if every pet parent heard about their dog's day?"
- ~~"Meet TailWag: AI Communication for Daycares"~~ → "More reviews, fewer no-shows — on autopilot"

---

## The Rule of Thumb

Before publishing any TailWag content, ask: **"Does the first thing someone reads describe what they GET, or how the engine works?"**

If it describes the engine — rewrite it.
