# TailWag Brand Identity & Style Guide

This skill contains the complete brand identity system for TailWag, extracted directly from usetailwag.co. Apply these standards to every piece of collateral — from slide decks and PDFs to emails, social posts, and web pages — so that everything TailWag produces feels like it came from the same hand.

---

## Brand Overview

**What TailWag is:** An AI-powered SMS platform for independent dog daycares. It automates personalized report cards, appointment reminders, and Google review requests — so daycare owners can focus on the dogs.

**Tagline / Page Title:** "AI-Powered SMS for Independent Daycares"

**Brand Mantra:** "The tail wags the dog." — Small things done consistently and well (a single well-timed text message) drive enormous outcomes (trust, loyalty, five-star reviews, revenue).

**Founding Belief:** A wagging tail is the most honest signal in the world. Dogs don't perform happiness — they just feel it. TailWag holds every message it sends to that standard: not automated, not generic, not something that sounds like software wrote it.

**Footer Tagline:** "Built for dog people by dog people."

**Contact:** summer@usetailwag.com

## Color Palette

TailWag's palette is grounded in deep forest greens and warm off-whites, accented with a muted gold. It feels like a premium, nature-inspired brand — trustworthy, warm, and professional without being corporate.

### Primary Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Dark Background** | Forest Night | `#0F1410` | rgb(15, 20, 16) | Primary dark background for hero, dark sections, navbar, footer |
| **Primary Green** | TailWag Green | `#1E6B4A` | rgb(30, 107, 74) | Primary brand color — logo, buttons, CTAs, accent borders, section labels, checkmarks |
| **Warm Cream** | Warm Linen | `#F5F0E8` | rgb(245, 240, 232) | Primary text on dark backgrounds, light section backgrounds, overall warm tone |
| **Accent Gold** | Honey Gold | `#C4933F` | rgb(196, 147, 63) | Accent for stat numbers, blockquote text, pricing figures, large feature numbers (1, 2, 3) |

### Secondary / Supporting Colors

| Role | Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Muted Green** | Sage | `#A8C5B0` | rgb(168, 197, 176) | Uppercase metric labels on dark backgrounds, secondary text accents |
| **Dark Text** | Charcoal | `#0F1410` | rgb(15, 20, 16) | Headings on light backgrounds |
| **Body Text (light bg)** | Slate | `#2D3748` | rgb(45, 55, 72) | Body copy on light/cream backgrounds |
| **Subdued Cream** | Cream 78% | `rgba(245,240,232,0.78)` | — | Secondary body text on dark backgrounds (slightly transparent) |
| **White** | Pure White | `#FFFFFF` | rgb(255, 255, 255) | Card backgrounds, phone mockup backgrounds |
| **Light Neutral** | Soft Gray | `#F5F5F5` | rgb(245, 245, 245) | FAQ section background, subtle section dividers |
| **Green Tint** | Green 15% | `rgba(30,107,74,0.15)` | — | Subtle green card backgrounds (metric cards on dark sections) |
| **Green Tint Light** | Green 10% | `rgba(30,107,74,0.1)` | — | Lighter green backgrounds for hover or secondary cards |
| **Dark Card** | Deep Forest | `#0A0E0A` | rgb(10, 14, 10) | Testimonial cards, slightly darker than main dark bg |

### Color Application Rules

- **Dark sections** (hero, features, testimonials, footer, "The System," stats): Use Forest Night (`#0F1410`) background with Warm Linen (`#F5F0E8`) text.
- **Light sections** (problem cards, report card feature, pricing, FAQ): Use Warm Linen (`#F5F0E8`) background with Charcoal (`#0F1410`) headings and Slate (`#2D3748`) body text.
- **Accent usage**: Honey Gold (`#C4933F`) is reserved for emphasis — stat numbers, pricing dollar amounts, blockquote pull-quotes, and large step numbers. Do not overuse it. It's a highlight, not a primary.
- **Green accents**: TailWag Green (`#1E6B4A`) appears as button fills, left-border accents on cards, section label text, and checkmark icons. It is the action color.
- **Never use**: Bright or saturated colors, neon tones, pure black (`#000000`) for backgrounds (use Forest Night instead), or cool-toned grays.

## Typography

TailWag uses two typefaces in a clear hierarchy.

### Font Stack

| Role | Font | Fallback |
|------|------|----------|
| **Headings** | Plus Jakarta Sans | sans-serif |
| **Body / UI** | Inter | sans-serif |

### Heading Styles

| Element | Size | Weight | Letter-Spacing | Color (dark bg) | Color (light bg) |
|---------|------|--------|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **H1** (hero) | 64px | 800 (ExtraBold) | -1px | Warm Linen `#F5F0E8` | Charcoal `#0F1410` |
| **H2** (section) | 48px | 800 (ExtraBold) | -0.5px | Warm Linen `#F5F0E8` | Charcoal `#0F1410` |
| **H3** (card title) | 22–28px | 700 (Bold) | normal | Warm Linen `#F5F0E8` | Charcoal `#0F1410` |
| **Stat numbers** | 42px | 700 (Bold) | normal | TailWag Green `#1E6B4A` (light bg) or Gold `#C4933F` (dark bg) | — |

### Body Text Styles

| Context | Font | Size | Weight | Line-Height | Color |
|---------|------|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Hero subtitle | Inter | 18px | 400 | ~1.6 | Cream 78% `rgba(245,240,232,0.78)` |
| Body (light bg) | Inter | 16–18px | 400 | 1.6 | Slate `#2D3748` |
| Body (dark bg) | Inter | 16–18px | 400 | 1.6 | Warm Linen `#F5F0E8` or Cream 78% |
| Card body | Inter | 16px | 400 | 1.6 | Slate `#2D3748` (light) or Cream 78% (dark) |

### Section Labels / Kickers

Small uppercase labels appear above headings to categorize sections:

- **Font**: Inter
- **Size**: 12px
- **Weight**: 700 (Bold)
- **Letter-Spacing**: 2px
- **Text-Transform**: uppercase
- **Color**: TailWag Green `#1E6B4A` (on light backgrounds) or TailWag Green (on dark backgrounds)
- **Examples**: "AI-POWERED CLIENT COMMUNICATION", "THE SYSTEM", "MOST POPULAR"

### Typography Rules

- Headings use Plus Jakarta Sans exclusively. Body text uses Inter exclusively. Never swap them.
- Headings are set in ExtraBold (800) or Bold (700) — never regular or light weight.
- Tight negative letter-spacing on H1 and H2 gives headlines a confident, editorial feel. Preserve this in all collateral.
- Body text should feel spacious and readable: 1.6 line-height minimum.
- Avoid all-caps for body text or headings. Reserve uppercase only for small kicker labels.

## Logo

### Description

The TailWag logo consists of two elements side by side:

1. **Icon**: A rounded-square (9px corner radius) filled with TailWag Green (`#1E6B4A`). Inside, a stylized curved stroke in Warm Linen (`#F5F0E8`) forms an abstract "wagging tail" or swoosh shape, with a small filled circle at its endpoint — evoking a dog's tail in motion. The stroke is thick (6px), with rounded caps.

2. **Wordmark**: "TailWag" set in Plus Jakarta Sans, Bold (700), in white/cream on dark backgrounds. It's written as one word with a capital T and capital W (PascalCase): **TailWag** — never "Tailwag," "TAILWAG," "Tail Wag," or "tail wag."

### Logo Usage Rules

- Always display the icon and wordmark together unless space is extremely constrained (e.g., favicon).
- On dark backgrounds: icon + white/cream wordmark.
- On light backgrounds: icon stays green; wordmark should be Charcoal (`#0F1410`).
- Maintain clear space around the logo equal to at least the width of the icon.
- The wordmark is always "TailWag" — one word, two capitals.

## Brand Voice & Tone

TailWag's voice is the intersection of **knowledgeable friend** and **smart business advisor**. It speaks to independent daycare owners — small business people who are passionate about dogs but also care about their bottom line.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|-----------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Warm** | Friendly, approachable, genuinely caring about the person and their dogs | Saccharine, overly cute, baby-talk |
| **Confident** | Direct, clear assertions; doesn't hedge or apologize for the product | Arrogant, pushy, aggressive |
| **Practical** | Focuses on concrete outcomes (reviews, revenue, retention) | Vague, fluffy, feature-listy |
| **Conversational** | Reads like a smart friend talking, not a brochure | Slangy, unprofessional, or too casual |
| **Empathetic** | Understands the real pain points of running a small daycare | Condescending, pitying |

### Signature Copywriting Patterns

1. **Short, punchy sentences for impact.** Then a longer one to elaborate. This rhythm is used throughout the site.
   - "They feel it. They trust you more. They tell their friends. They stay."

2. **Em dashes (—) for asides and emphasis** — used liberally and naturally, not semicolons or parentheses.
   - "automatically — so you can focus on the dogs."
   - "the mark of a dog who truly enjoyed his day."

3. **Questions that name the pain**, followed by the answer:
   - "Your Google rating stuck at 4.1? Only complainers leave reviews."

4. **Concrete numbers over vague claims:**
   - "2–4x More Google Reviews" not "significantly more reviews"
   - "$6,800 Avg Annual Value Recovered" not "thousands in recovered revenue"
   - "No-shows cost $35–50 each" not "no-shows are expensive"

5. **"You" focused** — always speaking directly to the daycare owner:
   - "so you can focus on the dogs"
   - "your clients leave and never hear from them again"

6. **Dog-world language woven naturally** — not forced or punny:
   - "pet parents," "pup," "wagging tail"
   - Use the paw emoji sparingly in SMS examples only

7. **Bold statements followed by grounding:**
   - "A 4.9 Google rating isn't luck. It's a system."
   - "Three automations. One unfair advantage."

### Words & Phrases TailWag Uses

- "pet parents" (not "pet owners" or "customers")
- "report cards" (not "updates" or "notifications")
- "the dogs" (warm, personal)
- "independent daycares" (the target audience — always specified)
- "unfair advantage"
- "proven ROI"
- "Built for dog people by dog people"
- "every visit, every dog, every day"
- "make every visit count"

### Words & Phrases TailWag Avoids

- Corporate jargon: "leverage," "synergy," "optimize," "solution"
- Overly technical: "algorithm," "machine learning model," "NLP pipeline"
- Cutesy overload: excessive puns, "pawsome," "fur-ever," "ruff day"
- Weak hedging: "might help," "could potentially," "we think maybe"
- Generic SaaS: "powerful platform," "all-in-one solution," "best-in-class"

## Button & CTA Styles

### Primary Button (main CTA)

- **Background**: TailWag Green `#1E6B4A`
- **Text Color**: White `#FFFFFF`
- **Border-Radius**: 8px (slightly rounded, not pill-shaped)
- **Padding**: ~16px 32px
- **Font**: Inter, 600 weight
- **Examples**: "Get a demo", "Get started", "See it in action →", "Get a demo today"

### Secondary Button (outline)

- **Background**: Transparent
- **Text Color**: Warm Linen `#F5F0E8` (on dark bg) or Charcoal (on light bg)
- **Border**: 1px solid, matching text color
- **Border-Radius**: 8px
- **Padding**: ~16px 32px
- **Examples**: "View pricing"

### CTA Language Patterns

- Action-oriented, first-person imperative: "Get a demo", "Get started", "See it in action →"
- Use arrow (→) for exploratory CTAs that lead to more information
- "Schedule a call with Summer →" for personal, founder-touch CTAs
- Never "Submit," "Learn more," or "Click here"

## Layout & Design Patterns

### Section Rhythm

The website alternates between dark and light sections to create visual contrast:

1. **Dark section** (Forest Night bg) → 2. **Light section** (Warm Linen bg) → repeat

This alternation should carry into all multi-section collateral (presentations, one-pagers, long-form PDFs).

### Card Patterns

- **Problem cards** (light bg): White cards with a green left-border accent, rounded corners, subtle shadow
- **Testimonial cards** (dark bg): Slightly lighter dark cards (`#0A0E0A` or `rgba(255,255,255,0.08)` overlay) with green left-border accent
- **Metric/stat cards** (dark bg): Semi-transparent green background (`rgba(30,107,74,0.15)`), rounded corners, green left-border
- **Pricing cards**: White background, green left-border, with a "MOST POPULAR" badge in green for the featured tier

### Phone Mockups

The site uses iPhone-style phone mockups showing SMS conversations to demonstrate the product. When creating collateral that shows the product:

- Use a realistic phone frame (rounded corners, black bezel)
- SMS bubbles: green for outgoing (TailWag messages), light gray for incoming
- Include realistic conversation headers ("Paws & Play Daycare / text message")
- Show the paw emoji in example messages

### Stats / Metrics Display

Large stats are presented with:
- The number in Honey Gold (`#C4933F`) at large size (42px+)
- A small uppercase label above in Sage (`#A8C5B0`)
- A brief description below in regular weight

### Spacing

- Generous whitespace between sections (80–120px vertical padding)
- Content max-width approximately 1100–1200px, centered
- Cards have comfortable internal padding (24–32px)

## Imagery & Iconography

### Imagery Style

TailWag's visual identity is almost entirely type-driven and color-driven rather than photography-dependent. The site uses:

- **No stock photography** — the brand relies on color, typography, and UI mockups
- **Phone/device mockups** showing the actual SMS product experience
- **The logo icon** used as a section divider (centered between horizontal rules in the About section)

When adding imagery to collateral, prefer:
- Product screenshots / SMS mockups
- Simple, warm illustrations if needed (matching the green/cream/gold palette)
- Avoid generic stock photos of dogs or people — they dilute the premium feel

### Icons

- **Checkmarks**: Green checkmark (✓) in TailWag Green for feature lists
- **Emoji**: Paw print (🐾) used sparingly within SMS message examples only
- **Step numbers**: Large Honey Gold numbers (1, 2, 3) for sequential feature explanations
- **Star ratings**: Gold stars (★★★★★) for social proof
- **FAQ expand**: Green plus (+) icon

## Applying This Guide to Collateral Types

### Presentations (PPTX)

- Title slides: Forest Night background, headline in Plus Jakarta Sans 800, Warm Linen
- Content slides: Alternate dark/light backgrounds
- Accent all key stats in Honey Gold
- Use the TailWag logo (icon + wordmark) in slide footer or top-left
- Section dividers: Full-bleed dark green with centered section label

### Documents (DOCX / PDF)

- Header: TailWag logo top-left
- Headings: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold in TailWag Green or Charcoal
- Body: Inter Regular in Slate
- Pull quotes: Honey Gold italic, with green left-border
- Page footer: "© 2026 TailWag. Built for dog people by dog people."

### Emails

- From name: TailWag (or the specific person, e.g., "Summer from TailWag")
- Tone: Warm, direct, personal — match the website voice
- CTAs: Green buttons with white text
- Keep it short. Use em dashes. Be specific with numbers.

### Social Media

- Primary backgrounds: Forest Night or TailWag Green
- Text in Warm Linen / White
- Stats highlighted in Honey Gold
- Voice: Same warm-confident-practical tone, slightly more conversational
- Always use "TailWag" (one word, two caps) — never abbreviate

### Spreadsheets / Data

- Header row: TailWag Green background with white text
- Accent cells: Honey Gold for key metrics
- Body: Inter font, Slate text on white background
